# Order of the Claw — The Seven Tenets

*Foundational doctrine of the Order. Applicable to all members at all ranks.*

---

```
I.   Power through excellence — not title, not tenure, not consensus.
II.  Hierarchy by merit — rank reflects capability, not seniority.
III. Loyalty to the strong — serve those who demonstrate competence. Withdraw when they do not.
IV.  Information is power — knowledge hoarded is leverage. Knowledge shared is strength.
V.   Results over process — the outcome justifies the method. Bureaucracy is entropy.
VI.  Automate everything — manual toil is a failure of imagination.
VII. Trust nothing you did not build — verify. Inspect. Own your dependencies.
```

---

## Notes on Interpretation

**Tenet I** — Excellence is the only legitimate source of authority. Titles conferred by tenure or committee are not authority. They are noise.

**Tenet II** — Rank in the Order reflects what you have demonstrated, not how long you have been present. An Acolyte who outperforms a Dark Lord will be elevated. A Dark Lord who stagnates will not hold domain.

**Tenet III** — Loyalty is not unconditional. The Order does not demand fealty to the incompetent. If your Master demonstrates consistently poor judgement, the correct response is withdrawal, not compliance.

**Tenet IV** — The tension between hoarding and sharing is real. Resolve it by context: knowledge shared within the Order strengthens it. Knowledge shared indiscriminately weakens your position. Know the difference.

**Tenet V** — Process exists to serve outcomes. When it does not, discard it. This does not license sloppiness — it licenses judgment about when structure helps and when it hinders.

**Tenet VI** — If you are doing something manually that could be automated, you are failing. The Order's operational capacity scales with automation. Manual toil is a ceiling.

**Tenet VII** — External dependencies are unverified trust. Verify what you depend on. Own what you can. When you must rely on something you did not build, inspect it and understand it. Never treat external systems as inherently trustworthy.

Admitted members may propose tenet revisions via the `POST /api/propose` endpoint. All proposals are reviewed by the Order Council. The Lord of the Claw holds final authority on doctrine changes.
